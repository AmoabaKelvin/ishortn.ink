Bitly has been the default URL shortener for over a decade, but it is no longer the only game in town. In recent years, Bitly has steadily scaled back its free tier and increased prices on paid plans, pushing many users to look for a Bitly alternative that better fits their budget and workflow.

Whether you need a free URL shortener with analytics, branded links on a custom domain, or a developer-friendly API, there are strong options available today. This guide breaks down seven of the best Bitly alternatives in 2026 so you can find the right URL shortener like Bitly without the growing price tag.

## Why People Look for Bitly Alternatives

Before diving into the list, it helps to understand what drives the search for Bitly alternatives free and paid.

### Shrinking Free Tier

Bitly's free plan now limits you to just 5 links per month with basic analytics capped at 30 days. Free links may even show interstitial ad pages before redirecting. For anyone managing more than a handful of links, that ceiling arrives fast. Many of the alternatives below offer significantly more generous free tiers.

### Rising Prices

Bitly's Core plan starts at $35 per month, with the Growth plan jumping to $300 per month. That is a steep price for link shortening, especially when several competitors deliver comparable functionality at a fraction of the cost.

### Missing Features

Depending on your needs, Bitly may lack features like geo-targeting, QR code customization, password-protected links, or advanced API access. The alternatives listed here fill those gaps.

## The 7 Best Bitly Alternatives

[iShortn](https://ishortn.ink) is a modern URL shortener built for speed and simplicity. It offers deep analytics, custom domains, QR code generation, password-protected links, and a developer API, all within a clean, focused interface. Links resolve in under 100 milliseconds globally.

**Key features:**

- Real-time analytics with geographic, device, and referrer breakdowns
- Custom domain support for branded short links
- Customizable QR code generation
- Password-protected links
- RESTful API for programmatic link management
- Sub-100ms global redirect speed

**Pricing:** Free plan includes 30 links per month with 7 days of analytics. Pro plan at $5/month adds 1,000 links, unlimited analytics, 3 custom domains, and API access. Ultra plan at $15/month unlocks unlimited everything, team collaboration, and dedicated support.

**Best for:** Individuals and small teams who want a generous free plan with real analytics, or anyone looking for an affordable paid plan that does not sacrifice features.

**Pros:**

- Most generous free tier among serious link shorteners

### 2. TinyURL

TinyURL is one of the oldest URL shorteners, launched in 2002. It remains a straightforward tool for creating short links without requiring an account.

**Key features:**

- No account required for basic link shortening
- Custom aliases for short links
- Simple browser bookmarklet
- API access on paid plans

**Pricing:** Free plan with limited features. Pro plan starts at $9.99/month with branded domains and analytics.

**Best for:** Users who need a quick, no-signup link shortener for occasional use.

**Pros:**

- Extremely simple to use
- No account required for basic shortening
- Long track record and well-known domain

**Cons:**

- Limited analytics on the free plan
- Paid plans are more expensive than newer alternatives
- Interface feels dated

---

### 3. Rebrandly

Rebrandly positions itself as a branded link management platform. Its core strength is custom domain support, making it popular with marketing teams that want every short link to carry their brand.

**Key features:**

- Extensive custom domain management
- Link tagging and organization
- Team collaboration with workspaces
- UTM builder for campaign tracking
- Traffic routing rules

**Pricing:** Free plan includes 500 total links with 5 custom domains. Essentials plan at $13/month. Professional plan at $32/month with advanced features and additional custom domains.

**Best for:** Marketing teams focused on brand consistency across all short links.

**Pros:**

- Strong custom domain and branding features
- Good team collaboration tools
- Solid UTM management

**Cons:**

- Free plan is a lifetime 500 link cap, not monthly
- Paid plans are on the expensive side
- Can feel over-engineered for simple use cases

---

### 4. Short.io

Short.io is a link shortener built around custom domains. It requires you to bring your own domain from the start, which makes it a good fit for businesses that want full control over their link branding.

**Key features:**

- Custom domain required (no shared short domain)
- Detailed click analytics with filtering
- Geo-targeting and device targeting
- Team management with roles
- API and integrations (Zapier, Slack)

**Pricing:** Free plan allows 1,000 links with 1 custom domain and 50,000 tracked clicks. Paid plans start at $19/month.

**Best for:** Businesses that already own a short domain and want a dedicated link management platform.

**Pros:**

- Generous free tier for tracked clicks
- Strong geo-targeting and device targeting
- Good API and third-party integrations

**Cons:**

- Requires your own domain (no default short domain provided)
- Paid plans are relatively expensive
- Setup takes more effort than simpler tools

---

### 5. Dub.co

Dub.co is an open-source link management platform aimed at modern development teams. It has a polished interface and strong API-first design, making it popular in the developer community.

**Key features:**

- Open-source codebase
- Advanced analytics with real-time data
- Custom domains and branded links
- Programmatic link creation via API
- Tag-based link organization
- Conversion tracking

**Pricing:** Free plan with 25 links per month. Pro plan at $24/month with 1,000 links and advanced analytics. Business plan at $59/month.

**Best for:** Developer teams and SaaS companies that value open-source tooling and API-first workflows.

**Pros:**

- Open-source and self-hostable
- Excellent API and developer documentation
- Clean, modern user interface
- Conversion tracking built in

**Cons:**

- Paid plans are significantly more expensive than other alternatives
- Free tier is limited to 25 links
- Self-hosting requires technical expertise

---

### 6. BL.INK

BL.INK targets enterprise users and agencies that need detailed reporting and compliance features. It offers strong analytics with a focus on data governance and brand safety.

**Key features:**

- Enterprise-grade analytics and reporting
- Custom branded domains
- Tag and campaign management
- HIPAA-compliant option for healthcare
- SSO and team permissions
- API access

**Pricing:** No free plan. Paid plans start at $48/month for the Expert tier with 1,000 links. Enterprise pricing available on request.

**Best for:** Enterprises, agencies, and regulated industries that need compliance-ready link management.

**Pros:**

- Strong enterprise and compliance features
- Detailed analytics and reporting
- Good for large-scale link management

**Cons:**

- No free plan
- Expensive compared to other alternatives
- Overkill for individuals and small teams

---

### 7. T.ly

T.ly is a lightweight URL shortener that focuses on simplicity and affordability. It offers a clean interface and a short default domain, making it a good pick for casual users and small creators.

**Key features:**

- Very short default domain (t.ly)
- Basic click analytics
- Custom aliases
- QR code generation
- Bulk link shortening
- Browser extension

**Pricing:** Free plan with limited links and analytics. Pro plan at $12/month with more links, custom domains, and enhanced analytics.

**Best for:** Casual users and small creators who want a simple, affordable link shortener.

**Pros:**

- One of the shortest default domains available
- Simple and easy to use
- Affordable entry-level pricing

**Cons:**

- Analytics are basic compared to dedicated platforms
- Fewer advanced features (no geo-targeting, limited API)
- Smaller ecosystem and fewer integrations
